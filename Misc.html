Transportation

Shuttle Bus

University Transportation offers several Bus Routes connecting the campus. The system generally operates during the fall and spring semesters when classes are in session from 7 a.m. to 12 midnight on weekdays and 10 a.m. to 12 midnight on weekends. View a Real Time Map of bus locations. The link also includes route-specific schedules.

SafeRide

Safe Ride vans provide all overnight service. Safe Ride is an on-demand, shared ride service which is available for transportation to and from NYU facilities during the Academic Term from 12 midnight to 6:30am. Learn more about SafeRide. SafeRide can be called using both the Transloc or NYU Mobile app.

Game Centers

Tisch Game Center

Tons of titles, both old school and new. The Tisch Game Center is open to students from any school of NYU. Their busy schedule includes lectures, workshops, exhibitions, tournaments, playtests, and many other strange and wonderful events. Most of these events are free and open to the public

Magnet Game Center

Rigged with Gaming PC's and every console you could possibly imagine from the past 30 years, Magnet Game Center has it all. The boardgame collection is also hefty, along with the constantly changing roster of arcade machines. However, Magnet is only open to Integrated Digital Media students at NYU Tandon on most days. However, Thursdays nights around 7 PM, they open their doors for all students from any school of NYU, and usually order pizza for everyone. Their doors are also usually open to anybody when they hold events for new game titles or talks from famous game designers in the field.

Software Resources

Student Technology Centers

NYU IT Student Technology Centers offer a large range of software for use by anyone with access to the facilities. Click here to explore the current software offerings by location.

Digital Studio

The Digital Studio, located on the fifth floor of Bobst Library, offers a collection of computer software and hardware for use by NYU community members working on a wide range of instructional and research projects. Software at the Studio includes applications for audio recording and podcasting, digital video editing, film and document scanning, and more.

Data Services

Data Services provides a variety of software for statistical, qualitative, and GIS analysis of social science data, and staff conduct free lectures and workshops in the theory and programming of statistical packages. Geographic Information System (GIS) software enables maps and spatial data to be created, stored, and studied using personal computers and servers. Any file with addresses or a latitude/longitude field — Census data, for example — would greatly benefit from a spatial visualization or analysis.

Virtual Computer Lab (VCL)

The Virtual Computer Lab (VCL) is an NYU service that provides students with access to "virtualized" applications — software that is hosted remotely but can be accessed via an Internet connection from an NYU student's personal computer.

NYU Computer Store

If you can't find the software package you need at any of the University's labs or studios, or if you want to purchase a copy for yourself, the NYU Computer Store (726 Broadway) offers a wide variety of software for sale. Exclusive discounted NYU pricing is available on some applications.
